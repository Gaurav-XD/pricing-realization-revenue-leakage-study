# Methodology
## Analytical Approach
The model uses a bottom-up financial analysis approach:
Capture transactional data (time entries)
Apply financial adjustments
Evaluate cash realization
Diagnose leakage drivers
Classify risk
This structured approach ensures root-cause analysis rather than surface-level performance reporting.

## Assumptions

Standard hourly rates are fixed for the simulation year.
Write-offs represent negotiated billing reductions.
Collections represent actual cash received during the year.
85% realization is used as benchmark based on common professional services standards.

## Why 85% Benchmark?

In professional services firms, healthy realization typically ranges between 85% and 95%.
Below 70% indicates significant revenue leakage and operational inefficiency.
Using a benchmark enables objective performance evaluation rather than subjective judgment.

## Diagnostic Framework

Revenue leakage was separated into two components:
Discount Loss
Collection Gap
This separation allows management to determine whether corrective action should target pricing governance or credit control processes.

## Professional Relevance

This methodology reflects real-world pricing and commercial finance analysis performed in law firms and consulting organizations.

The project demonstrates structured thinking, financial modeling discipline, and applied pricing analytics.
